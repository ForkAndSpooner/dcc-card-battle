**Floor 1**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The First Floor / Garbage Dump Neighborhood 1\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** A condensed, organized underground version of the surface city directly above it (for Carl, the Seattle area) 2, 3\. It consists of a massive labyrinth of brick walls glowing with green lichen, tight cobblestone alleys, and abandoned buildings 4-7.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** The introductory survival tutorial 8\. The floor is set up as a giant grid of equal-sized squares, each containing four distinct neighborhoods 2\. It is designed to aggressively drown crawlers in low-tier loot, achievements, and beginner skills to prepare them for class selection 8\.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** The Belly-Rubbed Pug (a fast-food restaurant magically transformed into a saferoom) 9, The Goblin Workshop/Murder Dozer Bay 10, 11, and The Pig Ball Track Chat History.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Goblins, Dungeon Rats, Scatterers (giant cockroaches), Slimes, and Shadow Leaks 90, 126, 146, Chat History. **Named/Mini-Bosses:** The Hoarder (Neighborhood Boss) 12 and the Ball of Swine/Tuskling (Borough Boss) 179, 180, Chat History.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **Never specified or fought in canon.** Crawlers simply needed to find a stairwell to survive the timer 8, 13\.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** Initially, the floor featured "exploding bathrooms" which punished crawlers for opening occupied doors, though this bug was later patched 14\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 150,000 ornate, wrought-iron staircases scattered randomly across the floor that lead downward 15-17.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** The first floor is off-limits to live viewers. Crawlers cannot accumulate followers, views, or ratings; the universe only sees an edited highlight reel until the second floor opens 18, 19\.

**Floor 2**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The Second Floor 20\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** Visually similar to the first floor's labyrinth, but the floors are stark white and the walls are made of magically protected cinderblock covered in orange-tinted lichen 20\.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** Continuation of the tutorial phase, heavily featuring localized mob ecosystems and the introduction of the intergalactic broadcast 18, 21\.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** The Rev-Up Syndicate Base (a moonshine manufacturing facility) 199, Chat History and Dungeon Gymnasiums 22\.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Brindle Grubs (janitor mobs), Troglodytes (Pygmies, Bashers, Virtuosos), Unvaccinated Clurichauns, Danger Dingoes, and Kobold Riders 172, 189, 196, Chat History. **Named/Mini-Bosses:** The Krakaren Clone (Neighborhood Boss) 23\.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **Never specified or fought in canon.** Carl and Donut bypass the end-of-floor challenges by finding an early stairwell 24, 25\.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** The "janitor mob" mechanic becomes deadly here; if corpses are not properly destroyed, the dungeon endlessly spawns swarms of Brindle Grubs (up to 5,000 per quadrant) to consume them 26-29.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 75,000 stairwells, following the rule that the number of exits halves with each descending floor 15\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** Live viewing is officially activated, allowing crawlers to gain views, followers, and sponsorships 21\.

**Floor 3 (Book 2\)**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The Over City 30\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** A sprawling, medieval-style settlement built over a dormant volcano 31\. It features wooden-slat streets venting sulfurous smoke from the hot depths below, illuminated by a fake starry nebula and a crimson sun painted on the cavern ceiling 31-33.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** The introduction of intricate, scripted NPC storylines (the "volcano world" drama) and "Elites"—named NPCs with heavy plot armor that the showrunners actively protect 31, 34, 35\. It also introduces a day/night cycle 36\.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** Magistrate Featherfall's Quarters 69, Chat History and Grimaldi's Traveling Circus Chat History.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Street Urchins (deadly nocturnal janitors), Mold Lions, Krasue (floating undead heads), and City Elves 37-39. **Named/Mini-Bosses:** Magistrate Featherfall (Dark Cleric) 39, Ringmaster Grimaldi (Pestiferous Vine) 36, Chat History, and Heather the Roller-Skating Bear 34, Chat History.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **Never specified or fought in canon** by the main party to progress.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** The ruins are extremely dangerous at night. While village main roads offer some safety, alleys and ruins spawn deadly nocturnal mobs 36\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 37,500 stairwells 15, which can be located using concealed map legends at major intersections 40\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** Crawlers are forced to make their permanent Race and Class selections immediately upon entering 41-43. The Desperado Club becomes accessible 44, 45\.

**Floor 4 (Book 3\)**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The Iron Tangle 46\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** A massive, industrial subway and train-themed level. It consists of dark concrete tunnels with electrical lights flashing by, multi-level transfer stations, trainyards, and deep chasms 47-49.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** A complex, deliberately confusing transit puzzle. Crawlers cannot simply walk to the exit; they must navigate a massive spirograph-like network of intersecting, color-coded train lines 50-52.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** The Abyss (Station 433\) 49, Station 83 48, Jikininki Ghoul Generator (Station 12), Blister Ghoul Generator (Station 72), and The Royal Palace of Princess Donut (Personal Space) 493, Chat History.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Ghouls (Jikininki, Blister, Wrath), Cave Mudge Bonkers, Snakeheads, Hobgoblin Mechanics, and Pookas 53-56. **Named/Mini-Bosses:** Gore-Gore the Mantaur 57\.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **The Mimic Rex** (Level 90 City Boss) 58, 59\. Though not a traditional gatekeeper, it is the apex predator of the floor. **Key Mechanic:** "Chomp & Split." If a piece is chopped off, it grows centipede legs and scuttles back to heal the main body; crawlers must sever over 50% of its mass simultaneously 541, Chat History.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** High-speed train derailments 60, the bottomless Abyss 49, and mechanical Ghoul Generators that constantly spawn undead for every mob that dies on the floor 61\. The floor physically "flips" due to a structural exploit 62, 63\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 18,750 stairwells 15\. They are located *only* at stations numbered 12, 24, 36, 48, and 72 64\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** Stairwells are locked and **only open 6 hours prior to floor collapse**, explicitly preventing crawlers from descending early 51\. System-wide immortality protection for NPCs and employees begins 65, 66, and Personal Spaces become available for purchase 67, 68\.

**Floor 5 (Book 4\)**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The Bubbles 69\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** 1,172 isolated, dome-enclosed "bubbles" 70\. Each bubble is split into 4 distinct biomes (quadrants): Land (desert dunes), Sea (ocean), Air (floating islands), and Subterranean (stone necropolis) 728, 738, 763, Chat History.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** Disconnected, isolated ecosystems. Crawlers are trapped in a single bubble with roughly 150 other crawlers and cannot interact with the rest of the dungeon 70, 71\.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** The Sandcastle of the Mad Dune Mage, The Bridge of the Akula (Submarine), The Wasteland Castle, and The Necropolis of Anser 741, 763, 768, Chat History.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Dirigible Gnomes, Night Frights (explosive bugs), Nude Glabers (undead mole rats), and Water Robots 72-74.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **The Four Quadrant Bosses** (e.g., Ghazi the Mad Dune Mage, Bugbear Captain Chaindrive, Quetzalcoatlus, Lusca) 784, 800, 802, Chat History. There is no single "floor boss"; instead, crawlers must defeat the apex boss of each quadrant.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** Lethal sandstorms 75, flooding within the submarine 74, and claustrophobic, shifting glass mazes 76\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 9,375 stairwells 15\. A single stairwell sits inside the castle of each quadrant 70\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** Crawlers cannot leave their starting quadrant until its specific castle is liberated 69\. Furthermore, the stairwells do not become passable until **all four castles** within the bubble have fallen 71\.

**Floor 6 (Book 5\)**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The Hunting Grounds 77\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** A dense, tropical jungle that swallowed the ruins of the "volcano world" after a cataclysm 78\. It features a pristine High Elf Castle in the southeast 78, 79 and a fully-functioning 1950s-style metropolis (Zockau) in the north 356, Chat History.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** The Hunt. Outworlder tourists ("Hunters") pay exorbitant fees to enter the dungeon and hunt the crawlers for sport 78, 80, 81\.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** Zockau 78, The High Elf Castle ("The Sugar Cube") 79, 82, Fort Freedom (Naiad River Palace) Chat History, and Prepotente Town Number Four 83\.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** High Elves, Naiads, Odious Creepers, Funeral Bells (Mushroom guards), and the Outworlder Hunters themselves 352, 414, Chat History.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **Queen Imogen** (Country Boss) 82, 84\. **Key Mechanic:** Intangibility & Reflection. She is immune to magic and physical touch, and reflects fire. She must be hit with synchronized blunt-force attacks to the exact beat of the background music to take damage Chat History.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** Odious Creepers combining in the dark to form massive, rapid-replicating abominations 85\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 4,688 entrances 15\. At least one unguarded exit exists in Zockau, with others in the valley or hidden/guarded by bosses 86\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** Safe room access operates under deep-dungeon rules: crawlers must fill a "blood bar" by killing enemies before they are allowed inside 87\. The Guild system officially becomes active 88\. Crucially, Hunters are **not** protected by the system-wide immortality and can be killed permanently 81, 89\. Class upgrades are also strictly required upon entry 90\.

**Floor 7 (Book 6\)**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The Great Race 91\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** Crawlers spawn in glass pods ("fish tanks") suspended around a central pillar/abyss 92\. The actual course consists of several hundred kilometers of twisting, turning enclosed tubes acting as race tracks 93\.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** A forced vehicle race. Based on a cosmic fairy tale, crawlers must choose a biological or mechanical vehicle and navigate a massive, branching track filled with puzzles 93-95.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** The Pole Position Chamber 91 and pitstop saferooms located inside tunnel nodes 93\.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Specific enemies are not heavily detailed because the floor was broken almost immediately, but the announcement mentions paths guarded by dangers and complex math equations 93\.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **Never specified or fought in canon** because the floor was bypassed entirely 96\.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** If a crawler comes in last place during a heat, the survival containment around their vehicle is permanently removed 93, 94\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 2,344 entrances 15, 86\. These are stacked, glowing portal stairwells suspended in the center pole 92, 97\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** No backtracking is allowed 93\. Paths automatically close after 10 crawlers or a single party pass through them 93\. Ultimately, the floor is literally shattered and skipped when Prepotente exploits the level's "Prince Rupert's drop" structural flaw 96, 98\.

**Floor 8 (Book 6\)**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** The Ghosts of Earth 99\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** Painstakingly accurate geographic recreations of Earth (e.g., Havana, Cuba) 100, 101\. It looks like the real world but is populated by "memory ghosts"—echoes of the actual human populations from the exact moment of the collapse 102, 103\.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** The Card Game / Faction Collection. Crawlers collect "Totem Cards" (T'Ghee cards) representing monsters or NPCs, utilizing them in massive, chaotic deck battles 104, 105\.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** El Capitolio Nacional de Cuba 100, La Iglesia de los Olvidados (Church of the Orishas) 644, Chat History, and The FOCSA Building Chat History.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Memory Ghosts, Duendes, Ghommids (undead), and Pox Slugs 103, 106-108. **Named/Mini-Bosses:** Amayon (Demon Prince of Sheol possessing the Madre de Aguas) 109, 110\.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **The Reminiscence Hydra of Malicious Compliance** 685, Chat History. **Key Mechanic:** Adaptive Head Regeneration. The hydra possesses nine heads manifesting as crawler memories. Each provides specific resistances, and if decapitated, they instantly re-inflate unless the stump is immediately cauterized Chat History.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** Massive demon incursions pulling crawlers, buildings, and NPCs into Sheol/The Nothing 111, 112\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 1,172 stairwells (two stairwells per region) 113\. During Phase 3, keyholders and their squads teleport back to their starting area to unlock the descent 114\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** The floor is strictly time-gated in three phases. Crawlers are physically locked in their starting region for 14 days (Phase 1\) 113\. They must then obtain an exit key and assault a different geographic region (Phase 2\) 115\. Finally, they can only descend during Phase 3 with a keyholder's squad 114\. Furthermore, memory-items looted from the Earth simulations turn to dust upon level collapse 102\.

**Floor 9 (Book 7\)**

* **(1) Official/Common Name:** Faction Wars 116\.  
* **(2) Biome/Setting & Visual Description:** The subterranean, funnel-shaped dwarf city of Larracos, dug deep into the earth with layers of gothic architecture and a river of lava 262, 793, Chat History. It is surrounded by nine pie-shaped territories ("petals") containing flat, pre-dug trenches, muddy labyrinths, and outer forests 117-119.  
* **(3) Dominant Theme/Gimmick:** Massive military strategy and territorial warfare. Nine sponsored factions (mostly outworlder tourists and corporations) fight to conquer Larracos and each other in four distinct phases: Ceasefire, Ramp-Up, Open Hostilities, and The Peeling of Larracos 89, 104, 117, 120\.  
* **(4) Major Locations/Landmarks:** Larracos (The City of Dreams) 104, 121, The Fortress of Unyielding Power and Authority (FUPA \- Princess Posse Base) 122, 123, The King's Point (Bone Clan base) 118, Shanty Town 117, and The Reaver Castle Chat History.  
* **(5) Signature Enemies & Mini-Bosses:** Faction Armies (Mercenaries, NPCs, War Mages, Tourist commanders) 117, 124\. **Named/Mini-Bosses:** Feral Gods (summoned intentionally or by accident) 125, 126 and the Blood Sultanate Nagas 127\.  
* **(6) Floor Boss & Key Mechanic:** **The Sultana / The Blood Sultanate** 127, 128 alongside roaming Celestial deities 129\. **Key Mechanic:** If a crawler triggers the succession line (like Donut wearing the Crown of the Sepsis Whore), the Blood Sultanate must be entirely wiped out—including the Sultan and all family members—before the crawler is permitted to descend 127\.  
* **(7) Notable Environmental Hazards:** Relentless artillery bombardment 130, 131, The Nothing actively draining into the floor and tearing reality 132, and unkillable Feral Gods roaming the battlefield 129\.  
* **(8) How Crawlers Descend:** 293 stairwell chambers seeded throughout the floor 133\.  
* **(9) Unique System Rules:** Factions cannot physically fight each other until the crawlers arrive 134\. During the "Ramp-Up" phase, magic and technical skills are entirely blocked; factions must rely solely on hand-to-hand combat and non-magical artillery 135\. Crawlers generally avoid the war, but Carl subverts this by taking direct command of a faction 117\.
