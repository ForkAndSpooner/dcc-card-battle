**Book 1: Dungeon Crawler Carl (Floors 1 & 2\)**  
**1\. The Fast-Food Safe Room (The Belly-Rubbed Pug)**

* **Description & Importance:** One of the earliest safe rooms Carl and Donut discover, this location introduces them (and the reader) to the bizarre, salvaged nature of the dungeon’s architecture. The Borant Corporation literally teleported a complete, intact fast-food restaurant from the surface of Earth (similar to a McDonald's) and placed it underground, complete with a massive, colorful children's maze and ball pit 1, 2\. It serves as a crucial early resting point where Carl and Donut can read the dungeon tutorials and regroup 1, 2\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A brightly lit, slightly eerie 1990s fast-food restaurant playground with blue and white colors, featuring a large plastic tube slide and ball pit, inexplicably situated inside a dark, rocky underground dungeon cave.*

**2\. The Goblin Workshop / Murder Dozer Bay**

* **Description & Importance:** A massive, grimy underground chamber dominated by an enormous, hissing steam engine boiler that looks like a peeled baked potato with copper tentacles 3-6. It serves as the maintenance bay for spiked, tractor-like "murder dozers" 3, 5\. Carl uses his expanding knowledge of explosives to roll a cart full of dynamite into the boss chamber, destroying the steam engine, wiping out the goblin chieftain, and securing his first major boss victory 4, 6-8.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A dark, grimy underground workshop filled with heavy iron cogs and tables of greasy tools. A massive, towering steam engine boiler with copper pipes dominates one wall, while spiked, steam-powered tractor vehicles are parked in a line.*

**3\. The Tuskling Borough Boss Arena (The Pig Ball Track)**

* **Description & Importance:** The arena for the Borough Boss is a purple-hued, wide-open field lit by colorful lasers, resembling a massive 1980s roller rink 9, 10\. The floor is carved with concentric grooves that raise stone walls to create a tight labyrinth 11, 12\. A horrifying, giant ball made of compressed tuskling flesh rolls rapidly through these corridors 11-14. Carl and the Meadow Lark survivors are forced to coordinate and build a defensive "redoubt" to wedge the momentum-based boss against the ceiling, establishing their group combat dynamics 13, 14\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A glowing purple 1980s roller-rink arena with neon laser lights, featuring concentric stone walls rising from the floor. A massive, horrifying ball of screaming, tusked pig-flesh rolls rapidly through the tight corridors.*

**Book 2: Carl’s Doomsday Scenario (Floor 3\)**  
**1\. The Over City**

* **Description & Importance:** The main setting of the third floor, the Over City is a sprawling, medieval-style settlement built over a dormant volcano. It features wooden-slat streets that vent sulfurous smoke from the hot depths below, illuminated by a fake, colorful starry nebula and a crimson sun painted on the cavern ceiling 15-20. It is critical because it introduces the concept of intricate, systemic NPC lore (the volcano world) and showcases how the Borant Corporation genetically engineers and wipes the memories of the populations living there 17, 20-23.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A bustling medieval village with moss-covered stone buildings and wooden slat streets. Plumes of sulfurous smoke rise from gaps in the wooden ground, illuminated by a fake, colorful nebula starry sky and a crimson sun above.*

**2\. Grimaldi’s Traveling Circus**

* **Description & Importance:** A menacing circus encampment surrounded by barbed wire, featuring three glowing big top tents, roaring fires, and sweeping spotlights 24\. It is defended by mutated "mold lions," horrifying stitched-together clowns, and massive, folded-paper tattoo elementals 25-28. Carl assists the half-naiad Tsarina Signet in a chaotic, full-scale assault on the circus, destroying the tents to break the boss's magical protections and successfully completing the *Vengeance of the Daughter* questline 25, 28-30.  
* **Image Prompt:** *Three massive, glowing circus tents surrounded by barbed wire in a dark, ruined city. The encampment is besieged by a towering three-headed ogre made of folded paper and a giant, glowing sea serpent.*

**3\. Magistrate Featherfall's Quarters**

* **Description & Importance:** A multi-story, stone administrative building positioned directly above a public mall 31, 32\. Seeking to uncover the truth behind the murdered prostitutes and the corrupt 201st Security Group, Carl infiltrates the building and detonates a stash of hobgoblin pus and dynamite 31-35. The blast blows a massive hole straight down through the structure, exposing the dark cleric Remex, who had been secretly turning women into floating "krasue" heads to fuel a massive spell 36-40.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A grand, multi-story medieval administrative building at night, with a massive, smoking, glowing hole blown straight through its center from top to bottom, revealing the shattered floors and offices inside.*

**Book 3: The Dungeon Anarchist's Cookbook (Floor 4\)**  
**1\. The Abyss (Station 433\)**

* **Description & Importance:** A terrifying, miles-wide cylindrical pit plunging thousands of feet into the earth. Its walls are lined with hundreds of glowing, colorful portals and catwalks at irregular intervals 41, 42\. Trains bursting through the portals plummet wildly into the fiery depths below 43, 44\. Carl utilizes the spatial mechanics of the Abyss and its portals to try and dispose of the catastrophic Mimic Rex and Krakaren bosses 45, 46\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A terrifying, miles-wide cylindrical abyss plunging thousands of feet down. The dark, fiery walls are lined with hundreds of glowing, colorful portals, with a derailed subway train falling into the darkness below.*

**2\. The Mimic Rex Station**

* **Description & Importance:** What appears to be a large Iron Tangle administrative building is actually the apex predator of the floor—a Level 90 Mimic Rex. In its true form, its entire first floor yawns open as a 300-foot-wide mouth filled with jagged, person-sized teeth and a lumpy, train-sized red tongue 47\. As the deadliest entity on the floor, it forces crawlers to either engage in an impossible boss fight or creatively manipulate the train portal system to drop the mimic into the Abyss 46, 48\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A massive railway administrative building transforming into a monster. Its entire first floor yawns open as a 300-foot-wide mouth with jagged, person-sized teeth and a long, lumpy red tongue extending onto the train tracks.*

**3\. The Royal Palace of Princess Donut (Personal Space)**

* **Description & Importance:** Upon purchasing a persistent personal space, Carl and Donut are given a massive, empty, industrial-style room resembling an airplane hangar with gray, corrugated metal walls 49, 50\. This space acts as a critical haven from the deadly train lines of the Iron Tangle. It allows Carl to establish a crafting workshop to build his explosives and the Royal Chariot, while providing a safe refuge to hoard rescued crawlers and NPCs 49, 50\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A vast, empty, industrial airplane hangar with corrugated gray metal walls, a concrete floor, and a glowing, transparent doorway, lit by dingy blue light.*

**Book 4: The Gate of the Feral Gods (Floor 5\)**  
**1\. The Wasteland Castle**

* **Description & Importance:** Located in the air quadrant, this floating fortress is a jagged, almost-rectangular island of rock ripped from the earth, dotted with corrugated metal shanties. It is held aloft by five colossal dirigible balloons and enclosed in a shimmering magical forcefield 51\. Controlled by the gnomes, this fortress rains devastating bombs across the entire floor, forcing Carl and his allies to hijack a hot-air balloon to mount a dangerous mid-air assault to destroy it 52-54.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A jagged, floating island of rock and corrugated metal shanties, suspended high in the sky by five massive, brown dirigible balloons, completely enclosed in a shimmering, magical forcefield.*

**2\. The Necropolis of Anser**

* **Description & Importance:** The centerpiece of the subterranean tomb quadrant. It is an unimaginably colossal mountain of carved brick and relief patterns (including screaming pterodactyls) that rises so high it touches the shimmering dome of the sky 55-57. The interior is a massive, flooded labyrinth containing the ghosts of the old empire 58, 59\. Crawlers who enter are frequently trapped in the rising waters, making it one of the most perilous regions of the bubble 60\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A colossal, ancient stone mountain carved with ominous relief patterns, rising so high that its peak touches a shimmering, dome-like forcefield sky, sitting in the middle of a dune-swept desert.*

**3\. The Sandcastle of the Mad Dune Mage**

* **Description & Importance:** Situated in the desert land quadrant, this sprawling castle features four tiers of defensive walls built directly into the side of a mountain 58\. Its interior is a claustrophobic, shifting maze of hallways made entirely of translucent glass and sand 61, 62\. Carl and Katia must navigate these electrified, glassy corridors to confront the glass wizard Ghazi, securing a vital piece of the Gate of the Feral Gods 62, 63\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A surreal, multi-tiered castle built into the side of a desert mountain, its walls and incredibly tight interior hallways made entirely of translucent, shimmering glass that reflects the hot desert sun.*

**Book 5: The Butcher's Masquerade (Floor 6\)**  
**1\. The High Elf Castle (The Sugar Cube)**

* **Description & Importance:** A stark, perfectly white, square fortress sitting in a forest clearing against a steep rocky cliff, featuring four incredibly thin sentry towers at its corners 64, 65\. The castle acts as the primary venue for the Butcher's Masquerade, an inescapable party where the top 50 crawlers are forced to mingle with deadly outworlder hunters and the country boss, Queen Imogen, culminating in an explosive, multi-level siege 66-68.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A massive, perfectly square, stark white castle resembling a giant sugar cube, featuring four incredibly thin sentry towers at each corner, situated in a forest clearing against a steep, rocky cliff.*

**2\. Fort Freedom (The Naiad River Palace)**

* **Description & Importance:** A glittering fortress situated on a muddy, trapped slope leading down to a dark, mile-wide river 69, 70\. Signet orchestrates a stealthy, amphibious assault on the castle to assassinate the leaders of the Naiad Confederacy. The battle is visually spectacular, featuring glowing folded-paper and tattoo elementals emerging from the bloody, algae-filled river to tear down the fortress walls 70-72.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A glittering stone fortress sitting on a muddy slope beside a mile-wide, dark river at night, illuminated by magical traps and besieged by glowing, folded-paper monsters emerging from the water.*

**3\. Zockau**

* **Description & Importance:** The designated "metropolis" of the Hunting Grounds, built to resemble a quaint 1950s American downtown with brick buildings, electric streetlights, and a manicured park overlooking the ocean 73, 74\. This city serves as the initial staging ground where the outworlder hunters spawn and establish control 75, 76\. Carl and Donut make a tense, invisible infiltration into the city, narrowly avoiding the heavily-armored hunters prowling the pubs and streets 73, 76\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A 1950s-style American downtown street at night, with vintage brick buildings, glowing yellow streetlamps, and a green park overlooking the ocean, subtly populated by heavily armored, alien hunters.*

**Book 6: The Eye of the Bedlam Bride (Floor 8\)**  
**1\. El Capitol in Old Havana**

* **Description & Importance:** A dungeon-recreated version of the historic capitol building in downtown Havana, Cuba. The structure features grand halls and sweeping stairs, though the entrance doors have been shattered 77, 78\. Carl and Donut spawn here at the start of the eighth floor, only to be immediately thrust into a desperate fight against memory ghosts and a massive, destructive battle between the player-killer Quan Ch, the crawler Ren, and the Bedlam Bride's demonic forces 77, 79, 80\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *The historic El Capitol building in Havana, Cuba, under a dark, storm-filled sky. Its grand stone stairs are littered with shattered glass, while magical fire and green lightning erupt from its main entrance.*

**2\. La Iglesia de los Olvidados (Church of the Orishas)**

* **Description & Importance:** A rotting, octagonal, yellow church with a mossy tile floor and a caved-in roof that lets in the moonlight 81, 82\. Inside sit seven distinct piles of rubble acting as shrines to the ancient Orishas 83\. Carl is forced to repair the shrines to gain massive, run-saving magical boons from the Orisha ghosts, but the location is also the site of tragedy when the god Ogun violently smites the guard Anton 83-85.  
* **Image Prompt:** *The interior of a rotting, octagonal, yellow church with moss-covered floors and a hole in the roof letting in moonlight, featuring seven distinct piles of rubble acting as shrines to ancient gods.*

**3\. The FOCSA Building**

* **Description & Importance:** A massive, Y-shaped residential building with an open-air mall perimeter 86\. It becomes the epicenter of a horrific battle against the demon prince Amayon and his swarms of winged horrors 86, 87\. The structure suffers a catastrophic, partial collapse during the fight, trapping crawlers and allies within a maze of exposed steel girders and rubble as demons hail from the sky 87\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A towering, Y-shaped, mid-century modern residential building in Cuba, partially collapsed and smoking, surrounded by an open-air mall, a field of melting ice, and the gore of thousands of winged demons.*

**Book 7: This Inevitable Ruin (Floor 9\)**  
**1\. Larracos (The City of Dreams)**

* **Description & Importance:** A staggeringly beautiful, inverted funnel/cone city dug deep into the earth by dwarves, featuring layers of ancient gothic architecture, colleges, and a massive waterfall 88-91. As the central prize of the Faction Wars, its streets are populated by terrified NPCs and defended by a network of devastating magical towers 91, 92\. The city's systematic destruction—including the waterfall turning into a river of lava—serves as the tragic climax of the war 93-95.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A massive, subterranean city built as an inverted cone plunging deep into the earth, featuring layers of gothic, ancient architecture, college towers, and a glowing river of lava cascading down its tiered rings into the smoky depths.*

**2\. The Fortress of Unyielding Power and Authority (The FUPA)**

* **Description & Importance:** The primary stronghold for the Princess Posse and the War Mage Rebellion. It is a hastily constructed, highly functional medieval stone castle surrounded by a sprawling, muddy labyrinth of deep trenches, scaffolding, glowing magical shields, and composite-concrete bunkers 96-98. Carl commands the defense of their territory and coordinates the massive, multi-pronged "Operation Ruin" from this heavily bombarded location 99, 100\.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A hastily built but heavily fortified medieval stone castle on a flat grassy plain, surrounded by a sprawling, muddy labyrinth of deep trenches, scaffolding, glowing magical shields, and concrete bunkers.*

**3\. The Reaver Castle**

* **Description & Importance:** A bizarre, heavily bombed stronghold that resembles a life-sized, plastic 1980s action figure playset 101, 102\. Its interior completely eschews traditional rooms in favor of a confusing, constantly shifting maze of tight, honeycomb-shaped tunnels stacked up to the ceiling 103\. Carl's forces must navigate this claustrophobic labyrinth and fight off robotic sentry dogs to rescue Louis and eliminate the Reaver faction 104-106.  
* **Image Prompt:** *A heavily bombed, blocky, plastic-looking fortress resembling a life-sized 1980s villain playset, its exposed interior revealing a confusing, shifting maze of tight, honeycomb-shaped tunnels stacked upon one another.*
