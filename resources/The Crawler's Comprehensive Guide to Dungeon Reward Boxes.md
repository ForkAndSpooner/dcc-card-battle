**Benefactor Box**

* **Brief History:** These highly coveted boxes are sent directly by a crawler's corporate or wealthy sponsors from outside the dungeon 1, 2\. Notably, Donut received a Celestial Benefactor Box from Long Haul Biological Waste Management Solutions that contained the Tiara of a Thousand Lights, and Carl received various Bronze and Silver boxes from Valtay Corporation and the Open Intellect Pacifist Action Network 3-6.  
* **Brief Description:** Specialized reward chests provided by patrons that bypass the standard dungeon loot generation system 1, 2\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Bronze to Celestial (Even Bronze Benefactor Boxes often contain better items than standard Platinum boxes) 1, 2, 7, 8\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** These boxes uniquely contain items from the patron's home world or highly specialized, non-dungeon tech (such as Valtay neural enhancers, protective shields, or extremely powerful stat-buffing potions) 1, 2, 9, 10\.

**Fan Box**

* **Brief History:** Carl and Donut regularly received these boxes for hitting massive viewership milestones (e.g., one quadrillion views) or surviving highly-rated events 11-14. Donut famously received a picture of her former owner, Bea, in an early fan box, while Carl once received a "choose-your-own-prize" box that led him to acquire the *Dungeon Anarchist's Cookbook* 12, 14-16.  
* **Brief Description:** A loot box whose contents are directly voted on by the viewers watching the crawler's feed 11, 13\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Bronze to Celestial (Higher tiers require fans to spend actual credits to vote, mitigating troll prizes) 12, 14\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Contains a wide, unpredictable array of items depending on audience votes, ranging from incredibly useful (magical dresses, endless ammunition) to useless or mean-spirited gag gifts (a skyfowl sex toy, pictures of exes, or a literal tube of mace) 12, 14, 17-19. Once awarded, a 30-hour countdown timer begins to allow the fans time to cast their votes 11, 13\.

**Boss Box**

* **Brief History:** Awarded after every successful boss kill, Carl and Donut relied heavily on these boxes to gear up early in the dungeon 20, 21\. Carl notably received the Enchanted War Gauntlet of the Exalted Grull from a Gold Brawler box related to a boss encounter, and his Enchanted BigBoi Boxers from a Neighborhood Boss Box 22-25.  
* **Brief Description:** A specialized reward chest generated specifically for the crawlers who land the killing blows or heavily assist in defeating a boss monster 20, 21\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Scales with Boss Tier (Bronze for Neighborhood, Silver for Borough, Gold for City, Platinum for Province, Legendary for Country, and Celestial for Floor Bosses) 20, 21\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Contains high-value, persistent loot tailored to the crawler's level, including magical armor, weapons, explosive supplies, massive sums of gold, and powerful spell tomes 20, 21, 23, 25-27.

**Quest Box**

* **Brief History:** Awarded for completing generated dungeon objectives, such as "The Sex Workers Who Fell from the Heavens" 28, 29\. Quan Ch famously completed a quest in a manner that triggered a double-upgrade achievement, resulting in a Celestial Quest Box that granted him his overpowered flight cloak 30-32.  
* **Brief Description:** A standard reward box given upon the successful completion of a localized quest 28, 29, 33, 34\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Bronze to Celestial (Can be upgraded multiple tiers via specific achievements like "Hadji" or "Bandit") 35-38.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Offers massive cash payouts, unique armor (like Donut's Fang Caps), and high-level skill potions 39-42.

**Adventurer Box**

* **Brief History:** The most frequently encountered box type, Carl and Donut opened hundreds of these throughout their crawl to sustain their basic survival needs 43-46.  
* **Brief Description:** The baseline, standard-issue loot box of the World Dungeon, awarded for general leveling, basic mob kills, and minor achievements 43, 45, 47, 48\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Common (Usually Bronze, Silver, or Gold) 44, 46, 49, 50\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Provides the basic currency of dungeon survival, consistently dispensing standard healing and mana potions, torches, crawler biscuits, basic ammunition, and unenchanted weapons or clothing 44, 46, 49-52.

**Pet Box**

* **Brief History:** Donut was awarded a Legendary Pet Box the moment she entered the dungeon for being the first crawler accompanied by a cat 53, 54\.  
* **Brief Description:** A specialized box awarded to crawlers who manage pets or enter the dungeon with animals 53-56.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Bronze to Legendary 53, 54\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Contains pet-specific items, such as Pet Biscuits, Scrolls of Heal Critter, and pet store coupons 44, 46, 55-57. The Legendary tier can contain the "Enhanced Pet Biscuit," an item capable of granting an animal hyper-intelligence and an official Crawler ID 58, 59\.

**Spicy Box**

* **Brief History:** The System AI frequently awarded these to Carl whenever he used his bare feet to crush enemies or trigger explosives, revealing the AI's deep, uncomfortable foot fetish 60-64.  
* **Brief Description:** A bizarre, fetish-themed reward box given when a crawler inadvertently satisfies the System AI's sexual or masochistic proclivities 60, 64, 65\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Gold to Legendary 61, 63, 64, 66\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Often contains foot-related items, such as the Enchanted Toe Ring of the Splatter Skunk, the Enchanted Toe Ring of the Leprous Bandit, or specialized foot lotions and patches 66-69.

**Asshole’s Box**

* **Brief History:** Carl and Donut began receiving these early on for acts of extreme cruelty, such as mass-murdering goblin babies, unleashing catastrophic collateral damage on NPCs, or stealing shopping carts from the elderly 70-74.  
* **Brief Description:** A box awarded for committing war crimes, slaughtering non-combatants, or generally being a terrible person within the game environment 70-74.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Bronze to Platinum 70, 74\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Often contains joke items (like drink tickets to closed clubs), weird tattoos, or items that reflect the crawler's destructive tendencies 75, 76\.

**Hunter-Killer Box / Savage Box**

* **Brief History:** Carl received 34 Gold Hunter-Killer Boxes for orchestrating a mass slaughter of the Syndicate Hunters on the sixth floor 77\. The Savage Box is the equivalent for Player-Versus-Player (PVP) combat, awarded when a crawler kills another crawler 78, 79\.  
* **Brief Description:** Bounty boxes rewarded exclusively for killing humanoid participants (either outworld Hunters or fellow crawlers) 77-79.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Gold / Silver 77-79.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Hunter-Killer boxes contain a massive bounty of gold and the physical, severed hand of the specific hunter killed (which acts as a currency ticket for a specialized prize counter) 80, 81\. Savage boxes contain "PVP Coupons," which incentivize and reward crawlers for betraying and murdering their own party members 78, 79, 82, 83\.

**Lucky Bastard Box**

* **Brief History:** Awarded to Carl on multiple occasions when he survived certain-death scenarios through sheer, dumb luck, such as miraculously surviving Signet's explosive magical attacks 84-87.  
* **Brief Description:** A reward box triggered by surviving overwhelming odds or narrowly escaping high-level hazards 84, 86\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Silver to Platinum 84, 86, 88, 89\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Often contains luck-based gambling items, such as Casino Chips or "Dungeon Gold Rush" scratch-off tickets, as well as highly valuable, short-shelf-life Scrolls of Upgrade 88-91.

**Deity’s Box / Predator Box / Scourge Box**

* **Brief History:** Carl earned a Legendary Deity's Box after being stepped on by a god, a Celestial Predator Box for wiping out every single hunter on the sixth floor, and a Legendary Scourge Box for holding the highest kill count 92-94.  
* **Brief Description:** Extremely high-tier reward boxes given for interacting with the highest echelons of dungeon entities (gods) or for achieving the absolute pinnacle of combat milestones (Apex Predator) 92-94.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Legendary to Celestial 92-94.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Contains unparalleled, game-breaking items, such as Divine-tier back patches (The Scavenger's Daughter), Hell-Kissed Celestial Skill Potions, or massive Automaton crafting kits 95-98.

**Sapper’s Box / Mechanic’s Box / Crafter’s Box**

* **Brief History:** Carl utilized these heavily to build his explosive arsenal and combat vehicles, receiving them for inventing traps and utilizing workbenches 60, 62, 99-101.  
* **Brief Description:** Specialized utility boxes awarded for achievements in engineering, trapping, and crafting 60, 62, 99-101.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Bronze to Gold 60, 62, 99-101.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Supplies the crawler with advanced tools (like the Goo-Inator 3000), trap modules, proximity triggers, fail-safes, and raw building materials 102-106.

**AI-Tantrum Boxes (Hyper-Specific Named Boxes)**

* **Brief History:** The System AI frequently created bespoke, absurdly-named boxes to communicate its frustration, mock the crawlers, or complain about Syndicate court rulings. Examples include the *Platinum It’s Not My Fault You Fish-Headed Assholes Don’t Properly Program Your Quests Box* (awarded when Borant lost a lawsuit), the *Platinum Have You Goddamned Figured Out How To Use This In Conjunction With the Fucking Voodoo Book Yet?* box, and the *Silver Spam Box* (awarded for participating in deck combat as a minion) 107-110.  
* **Brief Description:** Discretionary, custom-named boxes awarded by the System AI when its virtual mind breaks or when it develops an attitude about specific legal loopholes or crawler exploits 111, 112\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Varies wildly (Silver to Platinum) 108-110.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Varies entirely based on the AI's mood. They can contain massive rewards to spite the showrunners (like free Personal Space and town upgrade coupons) or highly specific tutorial items (like The Filthy Little Crawler's Book of Voodoo or skill potions) 113-115.
