**1\. Goblin (Level 2\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor, common in grid tunnels, alleys, and workshops 1, 2\.  
* **Visual Description:** Small, green, and mostly bald humanoids with pointed ears, angular faces, and sharp teeth 1, 3\. They dress in leathery rags 4\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee fighters who use simple, crude weapons like sticks, small curved blades, or clubs sometimes affixed with pineapples 3, 5, 6\.  
* **Danger Level:** Extremely low 5, 7\. They lack physical strength and die from a simple jab and hook combination, or by being effortlessly picked up and thrown 1, 5, 8\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They appear in packs, clans, or crews running dangerous goblin machinery 9-11. They try to surround their targets and quickly cut off retreat paths 9\.

**2\. Dungeon Rat (Level 2 & 3\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor and early 2nd Floor hallways 12, 13\.  
* **Visual Description:** Screeching, hissing rodents that are significantly oversized, growing as large as half the size of a cat as they level up 12, 14\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 12\. They rely entirely on biting and scratching their targets 12\.  
* **Danger Level:** Negligible 12\. A single rat can inflict a momentary poison debuff, but they are incredibly physically weak and can easily be punted against a wall where they explode like water balloons 12\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They act as the initial "janitor" mobs of the first floor, swarming over fresh corpses like a hurricane to rapidly devour the remains 12, 15\.

**3\. Scatterer (Level 2\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor, heavily populating the Hoarder's neighborhood 2, 16\.  
* **Visual Description:** Giant cockroaches about the size and shape of a loaf of bread, filled with squishy white goo 16, 17\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 18\. They run along the ground and attempt to swarm and bite the crawler 18\.  
* **Danger Level:** Very low 16\. They can be effortlessly jumped on and crushed underfoot, crunching loudly like potato chips 16\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They swarm in massive numbers to protect their boss, pouring out of the boss's mouth in a constant, disgusting stream 19, 20\.

**4\. Scatterer Brood Guardian (Level 4\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor, specifically guarding the Hoarder's trash labyrinth 21, 22\.  
* **Visual Description:** An oversized version of the standard Scatterer cockroach, growing almost to the size of a small cat 21\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 23\. They aggressively charge, hiss, and snap with their mandible-antennae, attempting to bite the crawler's legs 23, 24\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low 25\. They are slightly sturdier than regular bugs and bounce back from standard blunt weapon hits, but they easily die to a few magic missiles or a heavy stomp to the head 18, 23, 25\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They mix in with the smaller Scatterer swarms, attacking aggressively whenever crawlers get too close to their boss chamber 20, 21\.

**5\. Brindle Grub (Level 2\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor corridors 13\.  
* **Visual Description:** Fuzzy, cat-sized worms or bugs with no legs, colored in brown and black 26\.  
* **Combat Style:** Gimmick/Non-Combatant 13\. They are the 2nd floor's janitor mobs, designed solely to consume dead, decomposing bodies 13\.  
* **Danger Level:** Zero threat 17\. They inch along at a turtle's pace and die instantly when smashed like pudding 17\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** If they detect a corpse, the dungeon spawns them endlessly to clean the area 27, 28\. They do not actively attack living crawlers unless provoked, but they will swarm by the thousands toward any fresh meat, inadvertently trapping crawlers in massive waves of bodies 29, 30\.

**6\. Troglodyte Pygmy (Level 2\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor quadrants 14, 15\.  
* **Visual Description:** Three-foot-tall, scaly humanoid lizards wearing simple loincloths 15\. They look like upright Komodo dragons with long, bent, kangaroo-like legs 15\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 15\. They wield crude clubs and leap at their targets, utilizing a highly venomous bite 14, 15\.  
* **Danger Level:** Very low 14\. While their poison is highly dangerous, their physical bodies are incredibly fragile, and a single solid punch or magic missile will vaporize them like a tomato 14\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They hunt in small packs and use their kangaroo-like legs to quickly hop and swarm their victims 15\.

**7\. Troglodyte Basher (Level 3\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor gymnasiums and workout rooms 31, 32\.  
* **Visual Description:** Human-sized, upright scaly lizard creatures that are twice as big as the pygmies 31\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 31\. They use their superior size and spiked clubs to batter opponents 31, 33\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low 33\. They require four or five solid punches to go down, making them slightly tankier than the pygmies, but they are still easily managed cannon fodder 33\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** Often found working out in packs inside dungeon gyms alongside their boss 32, 33\.

**8\. Troglodyte Virtuoso (Level 4\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor gymnasiums 31, 32\.  
* **Visual Description:** Small, three-foot-tall upright lizard creatures, identical in size and general appearance to the pygmies 31\.  
* **Combat Style:** Ranged/Whip 31\. They can shoot their thin, wet tongues up to 15 feet to sting like a whip and immediately inflict poison 31\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low 31\. They are just as squishy as the pygmies, meaning their only real threat is the poison application from afar 31\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They stand in the back lines of the gym rooms, supporting the larger Bashers in combat 31, 32\.

**9\. Bad Llama (Level 3\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor alleys 34\.  
* **Visual Description:** Scorched, tan-colored llamas with giant yellow buck teeth and faces that drip with black and red ichor 34, 35\.  
* **Combat Style:** Ranged 34\. They spit baseball-sized red balls of venom that rapidly inflict the health-draining "Septic" debuff 34, 35\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low to moderate 35\. The Septic spit is nasty, but the llamas themselves are physically fragile and easily dispatched with ranged magic 35\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They congregate in alleyways and wait in groups to ambush passing crawlers 2, 34\.

**10\. Brain Boiler (Level 4\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor and 2nd Floor 36, 37\.  
* **Visual Description:** Floating creatures that look like a bizarre cross between a squid, an umbrella, and a brain, trailing tentacles beneath them 36, 37\.  
* **Combat Style:** Ranged/Gimmick 36, 37\. They use a psionic attack that causes debilitating headaches, completely bypassing physical defenses 36, 37\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low 37\. They move very slowly, like miniature blimps, and their squishy bodies bounce harmlessly like jam-filled kickballs when punched out of the air 37\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They drift together in slow-moving clusters and spam their mental attacks, but they drop highly prized ink pouches when killed 36, 37\.

**11\. Unvaccinated Clurichaun Rev-Up Consultant (Level 3\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor, inside the Rev-Up Syndicate manufacturing bases 38\.  
* **Visual Description:** Small, troll-like humanoids with oversized heads, hook noses, ruddy cheeks, and perpetually runny noses 38, 39\. They wear patched green overalls and buckled pilgrim shoes 38, 39\.  
* **Combat Style:** Ranged 38\. They use simple slingshots to continuously fire rocks from a distance 38, 40\.  
* **Danger Level:** Extremely low 41\. They die instantly to a single punch or magic missile 41, 42\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They work in large groups inside manufacturing rooms, sitting in circles playing dice or blindly operating the moonshine tub lines 39, 43\.

**12\. Laminak Rev-Up Consultant Elite (Level 6\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor, inside the Rev-Up Syndicate bases 44\.  
* **Visual Description:** Small, fat fairies who buzz around on hummingbird wings, wearing leaf-based pantsuits or dresses 44, 45\. They look remarkably like miniature, winged soccer moms with inverted bob haircuts 44, 45\.  
* **Combat Style:** Gimmick 44\. They are non-combat managers who are entirely immune to most health-seeping attacks and diseases 44, 46\.  
* **Danger Level:** Zero physical threat 47\. They can easily be plucked right out of the air and have their necks snapped with two fingers 47\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They hover in pairs or small groups over the Clurichauns, shouting orders and managing the Syndicate's workflow 44, 45\.

**13\. Danger Dingo (Level 5\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor 48, 49\.  
* **Visual Description:** Massive, mastiff-sized dogs sporting incredibly long, sharp fangs 48\. Their faces are covered in a black and white fur pattern that perfectly mimics heavy metal corpse paint 48\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 48\. They run down prey at high speeds and bite furiously 48\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low 48\. They take significant damage from basic magic missiles, falling over dead and steaming in mere seconds 48\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They run in aggressive packs, often serving as loyal mounts for Kobold slave masters 48, 49\.

**14\. Kobold Rider (Level 5\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 2nd Floor 49\.  
* **Visual Description:** Tiny, upright-standing creatures that share near-identical DNA with chihuahuas 49\. They wear beer-can-tab chainmail armor and tiny metal caps with a single spike 49, 50\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 51\. They wield long, angry-looking spears decorated with feathers, acting as lancers 51\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low 51\. A direct punch will easily shatter them against a wall, as they are surprisingly solid but very frail 51\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** Highly aggressive, fearless, and "yappy," they charge across the battlefield in coordinated packs atop their Danger Dingo mounts 49, 51\.

**15\. Street Urchin (Level 8\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 3rd Floor (Over City), strictly emerging at night 52\.  
* **Visual Description:** Squat, black, knee-high spheres covered entirely in retractable, needle-like spikes 52, 53\. They roll around the streets like tumbleweeds 53\.  
* **Combat Style:** Gimmick 52\. They are the nocturnal janitor mobs of the 3rd floor, rolling over corpses and debris to rapidly devour them 52, 54\.  
* **Danger Level:** Very low individually, but highly dangerous if provoked 54\. They only attack if their cleaning route is actively blocked or if they are struck by crossfire 54\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They gather in large, rolling swarms anywhere dead bodies pile up, easily overwhelming defensive barriers with their sheer numbers and sharp spikes 54, 55\.

**16\. Rot Sticker (Level 1-2)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st and 2nd Floors (often found on walls and ceilings) 56, 57\.  
* **Visual Description:** Small, round, black-hued creatures that look exactly like little raviolis 56, 57\.  
* **Combat Style:** Gimmick/Explosive 56\. They are living suicide bombers 56\. They run up, stick to a crawler's body, and violently detonate 56, 57\.  
* **Danger Level:** Individually weak and easy to kill, but their explosion inflicts the "Take Down" stagger effect and deals heavy blunt damage 56\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They swarm relentlessly, running up walls and across ceilings to drop onto unsuspecting crawlers in mass suicide waves 56\.

**17\. Shadow Leak (Level 14\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor, haunting specific dark alleys 58\.  
* **Visual Description:** Smoke-like, ghost-class mobs 58\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee/Elemental 58\. They swarm and utilize a stinging cold attack upon contact 58\.  
* **Danger Level:** Low, provided the crawler has basic magical weapons or gear to bypass their ghost-class immunity to standard physical damage 58\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They group up in dark alleys and swarm the crawler, providing extremely high, easy experience points 58\.

**18\. Green Slime (Level 2\)**

* **Floor/Area:** 1st Floor, typically found near the Hoarder's grid area 59\.  
* **Visual Description:** Basic, gelatinous green slimes 59\.  
* **Combat Style:** Melee 59\. They rely on direct physical contact to burn crawlers 59\.  
* **Danger Level:** Very low, but they require proper hand and foot protection to fight safely, otherwise their acidic bodies will easily burn bare skin 59\.  
* **Swarm Behavior:** They pool together in specific, localized sections of the grid to form hazardous barriers 59\.
