Here are the item cards for the Food, Pet Treats, and Recreational Drugs featured in the sources, ordered from the most prominently featured to the least.  
**Enhanced Pet Biscuit**

* **Brief History:** Awarded to Carl in a Legendary Pet Box just as he entered the dungeon. He fed it to Princess Donut, triggering her transformation from a regular show cat into an intelligent, talking Crawler 1-3.  
* **Brief Description:** A small, brown, hard treat with a crumbly texture. It is smaller than a fingernail 1\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Legendary Box Prize / High Value (worth a couple thousand gold to sell) 2\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Has over 100 different possible effects—half are good, the rest are neutral or decidedly bad 2\. It has the power to dramatically elevate an animal's intelligence and grant them a Crawler ID, but it also carries the risk of turning the pet into a terrifying monster (like a "toothed fleshbutcher") or teleporting them away randomly if eaten in a safe zone 2, 3\.

**Blitz Stick**

* **Brief History:** Initially given to Carl by Quint the pharmacist. Katia later developed a severe, debilitating addiction to these 4-6.  
* **Brief Description:** Mental candy that looks and acts like a licorice stick 4\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Black Market Drug / Highly Addictive 4, 7\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** A magically-addictive hallucinogen. Eating it temporarily raises Intelligence by 5 points 4\. Smoking it provides a 15% chance to permanently raise Intelligence by 1, but induces a hallucinogenic high that shows users non-existent memories, making it devastatingly addictive 4, 8\.

**Rev-Up Vitamin Shot**

* **Brief History:** Mass-distributed by the Krakaren to keep a whole floor's worth of train-riding monsters strung out, pacified, and moving in a predictable looping pattern 9, 10\.  
* **Brief Description:** A potent opiate and painkiller distributed in individual vials by Pooka bosses 9, 11\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Dungeon Drug / Quest Mechanic 9, 11\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** A race-specific sedative that gets the monsters high once they pass through a portal 11\. If the dependent mobs miss their dose and the high wears off, they suffer a severe withdrawal that causes them to physically mutate into much more dangerous creatures 10, 11\.

**Glory Bound**

* **Brief History:** A discontinued training drug that was used as a trap against Katia. She required a massive alchemical intervention to clear her system 6, 12\.  
* **Brief Description:** An incredibly potent, coma-inducing drug originally designed to help people learn difficult tasks through subliminal conditioning 12\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** High-Tier Addictive Drug 6, 12\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Puts the user into a coma, allowing them to fully relive a specific memory 6\. It severely damages the brain, is highly addictive, and will eventually stop working or outright kill the user 6\.

**Mairmei Mushroom**

* **Brief History:** The dromedarians fed these to the oblivious ambassador Leon (and Henrik) to control him and resurrect an ancient ghost 13-15.  
* **Brief Description:** An alchemy material consisting of small psychedelic mushrooms 13\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Rare Alchemy Material / Drug 13\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** "These little guys are quite the trip" 13\. Eating them raw makes the consumer highly suggestible, granting a rudimentary form of mind control to whoever is feeding them the mushrooms 14\.

**Mana Toast**

* **Brief Description:** A burned triangle of toast dispensed from a metallic toaster 16\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Free Safe Room Consumable 16\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Refills mana. The AI description explicitly notes: "That's it. Nothing more. Fuck you" 16\.

**Experience Cookie**

* **Brief History:** Found in early saferooms with limited services, acting as a small morale booster 17\.  
* **Brief Description:** Looks like a standard chocolate chip cookie 17\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Free Safe Room Consumable (One per crawler per day) 17\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Eating it instantly grants \+10 Experience Points (distributed across the party) 17\.

**Pet Biscuit**

* **Brief Description:** Standard dry animal treats 2\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Common Box Prize 1, 18\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Animals find them delicious; eating one feeds a pet-based class for a full day and helps placate them 2\.

**Creamsicle Charm & Travis Priest**

* **Brief History:** Two additional drugs Katia was addicted to 8\.  
* **Brief Description:** *Travis Priest* is a very strong painkiller, while *Creamsicle Charm* is made of ground-up fairy wings 8\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Addictive Drugs 8\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** *Travis Priest* is incredibly nasty and addictive, acting as a heavy painkiller 8\. *Creamsicle Charm* acts similarly to "shrooms," making the user temporarily forget their worries 8\.

**Trailer Park-Grade Meth**

* **Brief History:** Looted from a Bad Llama early in the dungeon 19\. Carl later traded it to a steam-engine goblin named Lorelai who happily consumed it 20\.  
* **Brief Description:** A small baggie of methamphetamine 19\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Common Loot / Drug 19, 20\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Acts as a highly addictive stimulant. Goblins value it highly because it is "dungeon made" 20\.

**Kitchen Upgrade Meals (e.g., Sausage Bagel Sandwich)**

* **Brief History:** Carl dialed this up after buying a Kitchen Upgrade for his personal space 21\.  
* **Brief Description:** Basic, warm meals retrieved from an upgraded food cabinet 21\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Personal Space Amenity 21\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Grants the "You are full\!" buff for the day, which gives the user a "Warm Tummy" that increases healing speed by 5%, decreases debuff times by 5%, and lowers all indirect damage by 5% 22\.

**The Orchid of Eileithyia’s Grace**

* **Brief History:** Found locked inside the Engaged Lock Box of the Night Wyrm 23, 24\.  
* **Brief Description:** A pink orchid flower meant to be eaten 23, 24\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Unique Prize 24\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Can only be consumed by females (or male seahorses) 24\. The system describes it as a metaphor for the duality of women's strength and weakness, granting a highly valuable divine boon 24\.

**Bopca Prepared Meals (e.g., Murgh Vindaloo)**

* **Brief History:** Prepared by Tally the Bopca in the first McDonald's-themed saferoom 25\.  
* **Brief Description:** High-quality, delicious, perfectly-spiced meals crafted by Bopca cooks 25, 26\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Paid Service / Safe Room Amenity 25, 27\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Provides excellent sustenance and acts as a massive morale booster 25, 26\.

**Crawler Biscuit**

* **Brief Description:** Standard dungeon rations for humans 28\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Common Box Prize 28\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Tasteless, basic sustenance 18, 28\.

**Uncooked Llama Steaks / Coconut / Pea Soup**

* **Brief Description:** Generic food items found in loot, shops, or food boxes 19, 29, 30\.  
* **Rarity/Value Level:** Common 19, 29, 30\.  
* **Powers and Abilities:** Basic sustenance. The Pea Soup became an unlimited option in food boxes after Carl earned the "Demons Hate This One Trick\!" achievement 30\.
