# How to Play — Dungeon Crawler Carl: The Card Game

## The Big Picture
You are a dungeon crawler fighting through the dungeon's floors. An alien game-show AI controls your enemies. It starts professional. It gradually loses its mind.

---

## Turn Structure

**Every turn:**
1. **Choose a deck to draw from** — Character deck (your crawlers, reliable) or Loot deck (random treasure, risky)
2. **Spend mana** to play cards and use abilities
3. **End Turn** — enemies fight back, chaos may occur

Mana starts at 3 and goes up by 1 each turn (max 10). Your sponsor may give you bonus mana.

---

## Playing Cards

**From your hand:**
- **Character cards** — place a crawler on the board. They stay until they die.
- **Loot box cards** — play for 2 mana to open immediately. Contains weapons, bombs, potions. Random!

**From your board:**
- **Click a character** to open their ability picker
- Choose an ability, then click an enemy to target them
- Abilities cost mana and may have cooldowns

---

## Abilities

Each character has 2–4 abilities shown in the picker with exact numbers:

- **Damage**: shows how much (e.g., "Magic Missile: 62 damage")
- **Cooldown (CD)**: shown if ability can't be used yet
- **Self/Team effects** fire immediately (no target click needed)
- **Enemy-targeted** abilities: pick ability → click an enemy

## Sponsors

Choose one alien corporation before battle. They provide:
- A **passive** that's always active
- An **Intervention** you can fire when you have 30 favor

Earn favor by killing enemies, using abilities, and playing well. The intervention button (purple panel, top right) lights up when ready.

| Sponsor | Best for |
|---------|----------|
| Borant Corp | Consistent (+1 mana/turn) |
| GlubGlub Beverages | Tanky teams (+8 CON to all) |
| Screaming Void | Aggressive (+10% all damage) |
| YumYum Meat | Long fights (kills heal you) |
| Princess D'nadia | Skilled play (damage scales with favor) |
| Phlegmaxx | Hard mode (healing bonus, weaker early) |

## Dungeon AI

The Dungeon AI narrates your battle. Early on it sounds like a smug corporate robot. Watch what happens after Turn 10. It starts to... break.

As **Instability** builds (shown in the mid-bar), chaos events start firing: enemies getting accidentally buffed, then debuffed, then the AI openly helping you because it hates its employers. By Turn 15+ it's making no pretense of professionalism.

---

## Leveling & Loot

**After every battle:** your surviving cards gain XP. Cards that died lose a level.
- Level up → +10 STR, +10 INT, +20 CON, +HP
- At Lv3+, higher-floor enemies become beatable

**Kill loot:** every enemy you kill drops a loot box. Tier scales with enemy strength (mobs = Bronze, bosses = Celestial). Boxes open at end of turn. Better loot = better items.

**Inventory** (🎒 button): equip found weapons and gear to specific characters. Gear stacks with levels and sponsor bonuses.

---

## Winning & Losing

- **Win**: Kill all enemy cards
- **Lose**: Your party HP drops to 0. Every card that dies in combat takes 5 HP from the party.
- **Floor progression**: Win 3 battles on a floor → advance to the next floor
- Deeper floors have stronger enemies, better loot, and a more unhinged AI

---

## Voice Commands (hold SPACEBAR)

You can talk to your cards. They know who they are and they'll answer in character. Try:
- "Donut, how do you feel about Mongo?"
- "Carl, are we going to make it?"
- "Hey evil Donut, I know you're a changeling"
- "Hekla, go berserk" (she appreciates this)

Rapport builds over time and unlocks their most powerful abilities.

---

## Tips

1. **Start with End Turn** — enemies only appear after your first turn ends
2. **Katia costs 2** — cheap early drop that lets you play a big card same turn
3. **Mordecai's Tactical Analysis** marks enemies → your next hit does 50% more
4. **Hold some mana** — Donut's Overcharged Missile hits 3 targets for 3 mana
5. **Instability is a second wind** — if a battle goes long, the AI starts helping you
6. **Sponsor interventions are clutch** — save them for boss fights
7. **Draw from Loot Deck** when you have excess mana but need an effect (bomb, heal)
