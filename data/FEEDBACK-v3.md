# DCC Card Game v3 Review

## 1. Adherence to Books: 7/10

You clearly know the source material. The flavor hits: Mongo's "Loyal to Mommy," Donut's "Hate-Boner" and "Royal Demeanor," Carl's "Cockroach" survival, the customer-service-with-menace AI voice, the sponsor system as mechanical buffs. These aren't surface references — they're mechanically integrated.

**Where it slips:**
- **Combat is the wrong center of gravity.** DCC is about *spectacle, audience manipulation, and clever exploitation of broken systems* far more than it's about 15-turn HP attrition. Your rapport and sponsor systems gesture at this, but the core loop is still "attack, attack, ability, attack."
- **The crowd is underweighted.** In the books, the audience IS the meta-game. Here it's one Event Deck card (Audience Vote) and a CHA multiplier. Carl's entire arc is about playing to/against the crowd. That should be a resource track, not flavor.
- **Instability is a great idea but tonally off.** The dungeon AI glitching at high instability is fun, but in the books the *crawlers* break the system through cleverness; the AI doesn't usually hand you wins. This makes the player passive in the chaos.

## 2. Fun Potential: 6.5/10

There's a real game here, but it's fighting itself. See problems.

## 3. Top 3 Remaining Problems

**1. The math doesn't survive the 1-300 scale.** This is the big one and it's still broken in v3. CON gives flat damage reduction of `CON/10 * 20`. Imani (CON 150) = **300 flat reduction per hit.** Meanwhile damage is `base + STR/10`, so Hekla's big hit is ~17–34. **Every attack against a tanky character does zero.** Mongo Smash (20 damage) vs a CON 130 enemy (260 reduction) = nothing. The 1-300 stat scale and the flat low-number damage formula are mathematically incompatible. You either fight 1-HP-chip-damage stalemates or someone gets one-shot. **You cannot ship this; do the spreadsheet.**

**2. Mana economy makes early turns dead and abilities samey.** Start at 1 max mana, +1/turn, cap 10. Turn 1 you play one 1-cost card. Turns 1–4 are slow ramps with no decisions. And because everything draws from one pool, there's rarely an interesting *tension* — by turn 6+ you have surplus mana and just dump your whole hand. The "one resource" simplification removed the decision instead of sharpening it.

**3. Rapport as cross-session unlock punishes new fights and breaks balance.** A maxed card (Bonded +20 all stats, ultimate, signature move) vs a fresh card is a different power tier entirely. In a long campaign your favorites snowball and bench-warmers rot (-2/session). That's thematic but mechanically it means battle balance is impossible to tune — the designer never knows which abilities the player has. Combined with leveling (+10/stat) and gear (+50), your stat scale inflates uncontrollably toward the 300 ceiling, making problem #1 worse over time.

## 4. Top 3 Best Things

**1. The Sponsor system is excellent.** Two resource tracks, real choices pre-battle, Interventions as climactic buttons, and it's *dripping* with book flavor. "Value Meal — sacrifice 1 ally, heal all others" is perfect DCC dark comedy. This is the strongest part of the design and should be expanded, not deferred.

**2. Choose-your-deck draw (Character vs Loot).** Genuinely good push-your-luck decision every single turn, reliable-vs-gamble, and it maps beautifully to the books' loot-box dopamine theme. Clean, replayable, thematic. Keep this exactly as is.

**3. Dungeon AI instability counter.** Even if it's tonally slightly off (see adherence), as a *pacing mechanic* it's smart — it guarantees long battles get weirder and prevents stalemates by escalating chaos. It's a built-in timer with personality. Good instinct.

## 5. Would I Play This?

**Yes — but not v3 as written.** I'd play it the moment the math is fixed, because the *texture* is right: sponsors, AI taunting, loot gambling, talking to your cards. That's a game I want at the table.

But right now I'd hit the CON wall in the first real fight and the whole thing would grind into a stalemate or a coin-flip one-shot, and I'd stop. **The flavor is shippable; the combat engine is not.**

## The One Thing To Fix First

Rebuild damage math before touching anything else. Concretely: either **(a)** scale damage with stats the same way defense does (`base + STR/2`, reduction as a *percentage* not flat), or **(b)** compress the stat scale to 1-30 and stop pretending 300 means anything. Defense as flat reduction on a 1-300 scale is the bug under every other balance problem. Do that spreadsheet pass and your fun score jumps two points.
